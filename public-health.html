Public Health Nursing Program

The Public Health Nursing Program at Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery is a specialized, professionally accredited course designed to train nurses in promoting and protecting the health of communities. Emphasizing preventive care, health education, and community-based interventions, the program equips students with the skills and knowledge needed to address public health challenges and improve population well-being with professionalism and compassion.

At Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery, the Public Health Nursing Program prepares students to serve as community health leaders. The program emphasizes health promotion, disease prevention, and evidence-based practice across populations. Upon successful completion of the program, graduates will be able to:

  • Assess and prioritize the health needs of individuals, families, and communities.
  • Use epidemiological tools to track, analyze, and prevent disease outbreaks.
  • Implement health promotion and disease prevention programs effectively.
  • Collaborate with public health agencies and NGOs to improve community wellness.
  • Promote maternal, newborn, and child health in underserved populations.
  • Advocate for environmental sanitation and safe public health practices.
  • Educate communities on hygiene, nutrition, immunization, and healthy lifestyles.
  • Apply ethical principles and public health laws in nursing practice.
  • Demonstrate leadership in managing health interventions and mobilizing resources.
  • Integrate research and data analysis into community health decision-making.
  • Respond effectively to public health emergencies and disasters.
  • Engage in continuous professional development and health policy advocacy.

Course Description

The Public Health Nursing Program at Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery is a dynamic professional program designed to train nurses as public health leaders and community health advocates. This program focuses on the prevention of disease, promotion of health, and protection of populations, especially in underserved and rural areas.

Aligned with the standards of the Nursing and Midwifery Council of Nigeria (NMCN), the curriculum combines public health sciences, epidemiology, community assessment, health education, and nursing practice. Students learn to analyze community health needs, develop and implement population-based interventions, and promote wellness through education and collaboration.

Through classroom instruction and community-based field experiences, students gain hands-on skills in disease surveillance, immunization campaigns, maternal and child health, environmental sanitation, and public health advocacy. The program cultivates leadership, ethical practice, and cultural competence to address the health disparities that exist within the Nigerian healthcare system.

Graduates of the program are eligible to be awarded the Registered Public Health Nurse (RPHN) qualification upon successful completion and licensure by the NMCN.

Program Duration: 1 year (2 semesters)

Award: Registered Public Health Nurse (RPHN) Certificate. Approved by the Nursing and Midwifery Council of Nigeria (NMCN)

Admission Requirements:

  • Must be a Registered Nurse (RN) with a valid NMCN license
  • Possess at least 1 year of clinical experience in a recognized health facility
  • Possess required academic transcripts and references

Program Highlights:

  • Focus on disease prevention and health promotion at community level
  • Community-based fieldwork and rural health outreach projects
  • Exposure to epidemiology, environmental health, and health education
  • Training in policy advocacy and public health leadership
