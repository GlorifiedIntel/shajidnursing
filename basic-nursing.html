Basic Nursing Program

The Basic Nursing Program at Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery is a comprehensive, professionally accredited course designed to prepare students for a successful career in clinical nursing. With a focus on foundational medical knowledge, practical skills, and compassionate patient care, this program equips future nurses to meet the healthcare needs of their communities with excellence.

At Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery, the Basic Nursing Program equips students with the skills and knowledge to deliver compassionate, professional, and evidence-based care. Upon successful completion of the program, graduates will be able to:

  • Demonstrate comprehensive knowledge of the structure and functions of the human body.
  • Apply pharmacological principles and safe medication practices.
  • Utilize the nursing process and critical thinking in care planning and delivery.
  • Deliver holistic, patient-centered care that respects cultural and spiritual needs.
  • Carry out essential nursing procedures and technical skills competently.
  • Promote public health through education and disease prevention strategies.
  • Communicate professionally and empathetically with patients and healthcare teams.
  • Exhibit ethical behavior and legal accountability based on NMCN standards.
  • Participate in interdisciplinary collaboration to improve healthcare delivery.
  • Demonstrate leadership in managing patient care and supervising staff.
  • Incorporate research and evidence-based practice into nursing interventions.
  • Prepare for national licensure and embrace lifelong professional development.

Course Description

The Basic Nursing Program at Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery is a rigorous, three-year full-time professional training designed to prepare competent, compassionate, and ethical nursing professionals. The program equips students with the theoretical knowledge, practical skills, and clinical experience required to provide high-quality nursing care across a variety of healthcare settings.

Grounded in the standards of the Nursing and Midwifery Council of Nigeria (NMCN), the curriculum integrates foundational sciences, behavioral and social sciences, nursing theory, clinical practice, and community health. Students learn to assess health needs, plan and implement nursing interventions, and evaluate patient outcomes while upholding the highest standards of safety, empathy, and cultural sensitivity.

Throughout the program, students participate in supervised clinical postings in hospitals, primary health care centers, and community health initiatives. The course fosters leadership, communication, and critical thinking skills, preparing graduates not only to excel in the national licensure examination but also to contribute meaningfully to the advancement of healthcare in Nigeria and beyond.

Graduates of the program are awarded the Registered Nurse (RN) qualification upon successful completion and licensure by the NMCN.

Program Duration: 3 years (6 semesters)

Award: Registered Nurse (RN) Certificate. Approved by the Nursing and Midwifery Council of Nigeria (NMCN)

Admission Requirements:

  • Minimum of five (5) credits in English Language, Mathematics, Biology, Chemistry, and Physics
  • Results accepted: WAEC, NECO, NABTEB (max. two sittings)
  • Candidates must be at least 17 years old at the time of admission

Program Highlights:

  • Modern laboratories and simulation rooms
  • Experienced faculty and personalized mentoring
  • Clinical placements in top hospitals and community clinics
  • Strong focus on patient-centered and evidence-based care
