Excellence in Nursing & Midwifery Education and Practice!Shajid College

The CORE mandate of Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery is to train and equip competent, compassionate, and professionally qualified nurses and midwives who can meet the healthcare needs of individuals, families, and communities. The college is committed to delivering high-quality nursing and midwifery education through a blend of theoretical instruction, clinical practice, and ethical training.

Frequently Asked Questions

To provide quick, clear answers to the most common questions prospective students, parents, and other stakeholders may have about Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery. The FAQ will reduce repetitive inquiries, improve user experience on the website, and assist in guiding students through the admission and academic processes.

What programs are offered at Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery?

Shajid College offers the following programs:
  1. Basic Nursing (RN)
  2. Basic Midwifery (RM)
  3. Post-Basic Midwifery
  4. Refresher and Continuing Education Courses

Each program is designed to prepare students for professional certification and practice in healthcare environments.

What are the admission requirements?

To apply for Basic Nursing or Midwifery, candidates must have a minimum of five (5) credits in English Language, Mathematics, Biology, Chemistry, and Physics in WAEC, NECO, or NABTEB, obtained in not more than two sittings.

What is the duration of the programs?

  1. Basic Nursing: 3 years
  2. Basic Midwifery: 3 years
  3. Post-Basic Midwifery: 18 months
  4. Additional training or refresher courses vary in length depending on the specific program.

Is Shajid College accredited?

Yes. Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery is fully accredited by the Nursing and Midwifery Council of Nigeria (NMCN) and operates in compliance with national educational and healthcare standards.
