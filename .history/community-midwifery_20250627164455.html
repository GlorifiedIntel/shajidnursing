Community Midwifery Program

At Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery, the Community Midwifery Program prepares students to provide essential maternal and newborn care in rural and underserved communities. Upon successful completion of the program, graduates will be able to deliver safe, culturally sensitive, and community-focused midwifery services.

The Community Midwifery Program at Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery is designed to train midwives who can deliver essential maternal and newborn care services at the grassroots level. The program focuses on community-based interventions, preventive care, and health promotion in underserved and rural areas. Upon successful completion of the program, graduates will be able to:

  • Provide maternal and newborn care within community and home-based settings.
  • Support safe deliveries using culturally sensitive and evidence-based practices.
  • Identify and manage uncomplicated pregnancies and recognize danger signs for referral.
  • Educate women, families, and community members on maternal and child health.
  • Promote antenatal, postnatal, and newborn care services in rural communities.
  • Encourage exclusive breastfeeding and essential newborn care practices.
  • Work collaboratively with local health workers and traditional birth attendants.
  • Support routine immunization and preventive health services for mothers and infants.
  • Establish referral systems for high-risk pregnancies and obstetric emergencies.
  • Respect cultural beliefs while upholding ethical standards and patient rights.
  • Conduct community health mapping and outreach for maternal services.
  • Contribute to national efforts in reducing maternal and infant mortality through continuous learning and service.

Program Duration: 2 years (4 semesters)

Award: Community Midwife (CM) Certificate. Approved by the Nursing and Midwifery Council of Nigeria (NMCN)

Admission Requirements:

  • Minimum of five (5) credits including English Language, Biology, and any other three relevant subjects
  • Accepted results: WAEC, NECO, NABTEB (maximum of two sittings)
  • Candidates must be at least 17 years old at the time of admission
  • Priority may be given to applicants from rural or underserved communities

Program Highlights:

  • Practical training focused on rural maternal and newborn care
  • Community outreach programs and health education
  • Clinical postings in primary health care centers
  • Supervised deliveries and postnatal care experiences
