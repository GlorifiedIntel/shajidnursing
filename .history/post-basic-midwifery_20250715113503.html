Post Basic Midwifery Programs

The Post Basic Midwifery Program at Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery is an advanced, professionally accredited course designed for registered nurses seeking to specialize in midwifery and enhance their clinical expertise. With a strong emphasis on maternal and newborn health, professional development, and evidence-based practice, this program equips nurses with the knowledge and hands-on skills required to provide high-quality, focused care across diverse maternal and reproductive health settings.

About the Post Basic Midwifery Program

The Post Basic Midwifery Program at Shajid College is designed for registered nurses who are passionate about maternal and newborn care. This two-year intensive course equips students with specialized skills in antenatal, intrapartum, and postnatal services, enabling them to provide safe, culturally competent, and evidence-based midwifery care.

The program blends theory with clinical experience across hospitals and community health centers, with a focus on reducing maternal and infant mortality. It also builds leadership, advocacy, and critical decision-making abilities essential for midwifery practice.

At Shajid College, our mission is to train midwives who are not only professionally competent but also deeply committed to improving reproductive and maternal health outcomes in underserved communities.

Program Overview

Duration

2 years (4 semesters) of full-time study, with hands-on training in both hospital and community-based maternity services.

Requirements

Registered Nurse (RN) with a valid license and at least one year of clinical practice experience.

Tuition

Affordable tuition with flexible payment options. Limited scholarships may be available based on merit and need.

Award

Successful graduates will earn the title of Registered Midwife (RM), certified by the Nursing and Midwifery Council.
