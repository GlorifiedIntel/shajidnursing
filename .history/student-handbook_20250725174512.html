Student Handbook

We are delighted to welcome you to the Shajid Royal College of Nursing and Midwifery. This Student Handbook is designed to serve as your essential guide throughout your academic journey with us. Whether you are beginning your first year or advancing your professional qualifications, this online resource provides vital information about our programs, policies, services, and support systems.

Our mission is to empower future nurses and midwives with the knowledge, skills, and values necessary to provide compassionate, evidence-based care. At Shajid Royal College, we are committed to excellence in education, clinical practice, and personal development.

Please take time to read through this handbook carefully. It is regularly updated to reflect the latest policies and resources to help you succeed. We wish you a fulfilling and enriching experience as part of our academic community.

How to Use This Handbook

This handbook is a comprehensive guide to the academic, clinical, and ethical expectations of students enrolled at Shajid Royal College of Nursing and Midwifery. It supplements official policies, which may also be found in the academic calendar, institutional bylaws, and course syllabi.

We recommend you consult this document regularly for:

  1. Understanding academic regulations and course progression
  2. Preparing for clinical placements and licensing exams
  3. Locating student support services such as counseling or library use
  4. Knowing your rights, responsibilities, and ethical obligations
  5. Accessing official forms, timelines, and administrative contacts

For quick reference, use the Table of Contents at the beginning of this handbook to navigate directly to relevant sections. Updates to the handbook may be published periodically; students will be notified through official channels.

Section 1: College Overview

1.1 Mission, Vision, and Philosophy

Mission: To educate competent, compassionate, and ethical healthcare professionals who will serve with excellence in communities across Nigeria and beyond.

Vision: To be a leading center for nursing and midwifery education in West Africa, advancing practice through innovation, research, and service.

Philosophy: We believe in holistic education that integrates theoretical knowledge with practical skills, rooted in respect for human dignity and the sanctity of life.

1.2 Core Values

  • Excellence
  • Compassion
  • Integrity
  • Lifelong Learning
  • Professionalism
  • Collaboration

1.3 Accreditation and Recognition

Shajid Royal College of Nursing and Midwifery is accredited by the Nursing and Midwifery Council of Nigeria (NMCN) and recognized by the Federal Ministry of Education. All academic programs conform to national and professional standards, ensuring our graduates are eligible for licensure and employment both locally and internationally.

1.4 Academic Programmes

  • Diploma in General Nursing
  • Diploma in Basic Midwifery
  • Post-Basic Midwifery
  • Post-Basic Psychiatric Nursing
  • Certificate Courses in Community Health & First Aid

1.5 Curriculum Foundation

The academic structure of Shajid Royal College is grounded in the Competency-Based Education (CBE) model recommended by NMCN and WHO. The curriculum is structured to ensure that every graduate attains clinical competence, critical thinking, and ethical sensitivity before licensure.

Core pillars of the curriculum include:

  • Theoretical Foundations: Lecture-based instruction in nursing sciences, behavioral sciences, and public health.
  • Clinical Practicum: Rotations across medical-surgical units, maternity wards, pediatric clinics, psychiatry units, and community health centers.
  • Skills Laboratory: Simulation-based training using mannequins and models to enhance procedural proficiency before real-life practice.
  • Research and Evidence-Based Practice: Training in data collection, basic research methods, and application of scientific evidence in care delivery.
  • Ethics and Professionalism: Emphasis on the nurse’s legal responsibilities, patient rights, cultural sensitivity, and communication.
  • ICT in Health: Basic computer training and introduction to health information systems and electronic recordkeeping.

Program delivery methods include lectures, tutorials, clinical debriefings, problem-based learning (PBL), peer reviews, and community outreach projects. Students are expected to actively participate in all aspects of the curriculum to graduate in good standing.

1.6 College Leadership and Administrative Structure

The College operates under a structured governance system designed to ensure academic excellence, transparency, and responsiveness to student needs.

Administrative Leadership:

  • Provost: Chief Executive Officer of the College, responsible for policy direction, external relations, and academic oversight.
  • Academic Dean: Oversees faculty affairs, curriculum implementation, and academic standards.
  • Registrar: Responsible for admissions, records, registration, and student documentation.
  • Bursar: Manages the College’s finances, including student billing, payments, and financial aid.
  • Heads of Department (HODs): Coordinate instruction and administration for each academic program.
  • Clinical Coordinator: Manages clinical placements, evaluations, hospital partnerships, and preceptor assignments.
  • Student Affairs Officer: Provides support on welfare issues, counseling referrals, accommodation, and discipline.

Regular administrative meetings ensure that student feedback and academic performance reports are discussed and acted upon. Students are encouraged to meet with relevant officials when seeking clarification, support, or academic interventions.

1.7 Student Representation and Participatory Governance

Shajid Royal College promotes participatory governance by ensuring student involvement in selected decision-making bodies and institutional forums. Student voices are considered essential to fostering a supportive academic environment.

Students are represented in the following:

  • Student Union Government (SUG): Elected student leaders who advocate for the welfare, rights, and interests of the student body.
  • Academic Board: Student representatives may attend advisory meetings where curriculum delivery and performance trends are reviewed.
  • Disciplinary Committee: Students under review may request peer representation during hearings.
  • Welfare and Hostel Committee: Student leaders collaborate with staff to ensure a clean, safe, and inclusive residential experience.

Representatives are expected to attend meetings, communicate updates to peers, and bring student concerns forward through appropriate channels. Training on leadership, communication, and advocacy is periodically provided to student leaders by the College.

Section 2: General Academic and Institutional Policies

2.1 Student Conduct and Professional Behavior

Shajid Royal College of Nursing and Midwifery is committed to the development of future healthcare professionals who embody the values of compassion, discipline, accountability, and integrity. As a professional training institution governed by the standards of the Nursing and Midwifery Council of Nigeria (NMCN), the College holds all students to a high standard of personal and professional conduct, both on campus and in clinical settings.

Upon enrollment, students become representatives of both the College and the nursing profession at large. As such, their behavior must reflect the ethical expectations of healthcare providers. All students are expected to demonstrate respect for their peers, faculty, patients, clinical supervisors, and institutional policies.

Examples of Expected Professional Conduct:

  • Regular and punctual attendance in lectures, skills labs, and clinical assignments
  • Polite and respectful communication with instructors, administrators, peers, and clients
  • Compliance with established clinical guidelines, hospital protocols, and college rules
  • Maintenance of confidentiality regarding patient care and sensitive health records
  • Upholding personal hygiene, modest dressing, and professional appearance
  • Demonstration of honesty in coursework, examinations, and clinical documentation

Unprofessional Conduct May Include (but is not limited to):

  • Cheating during examinations or forging clinical logbooks
  • Verbal abuse, threats, fighting, or insubordination
  • Absenteeism or tardiness without formal excuse
  • Possession or use of drugs, alcohol, or weapons on campus or at clinical sites
  • Disrespect toward patients, staff, or fellow students
  • Posting defamatory or offensive content about the College on social media

Misconduct will be investigated through formal procedures outlined by the Student Disciplinary Committee. Sanctions may include a written warning, suspension, academic probation, or expulsion depending on severity. Appeals may be submitted in writing within 7 working days of any disciplinary action.

2.2 Attendance and Participation Requirements

Attendance and punctuality are fundamental components of nursing and midwifery education. At Shajid Royal College, students are expected to engage fully in both classroom instruction and clinical practice.

Classroom Attendance: A minimum of 75% attendance is required for all academic sessions. Late arrivals may be marked partially or fully absent. Failure to meet this threshold results in ineligibility for final exams.

Clinical Attendance: Students must complete 100% of clinical hours. Any missed hours must be rescheduled and formally approved. Excused absences must be documented (e.g., medical certificate).

Participation: Students are expected to actively participate in discussions, presentations, group work, and clinical practice. Passive attendance may affect participation grades and academic standing.

Excused Absences: These may be granted for health issues, family emergencies, religious observances, or official school activities—provided that formal documentation is submitted within 48 hours of return.

2.3 Grading System and Academic Progression

Academic performance is measured using a combination of Continuous Assessments and End-of-Semester Examinations. Clinical placements are assessed via logbooks, supervisor feedback, and standardized evaluations.

Grading Scale:

  • A: 70–100% – Excellent
  • B: 60–69% – Very Good
  • C: 50–59% – Satisfactory
  • F: Below 50% – Fail
  • I: Incomplete
  • W: Withdrawn

Assessment Weights: Continuous Assessment – 40%; Final Exam – 60%

Progression: Students must pass all academic and clinical components and meet attendance and financial obligations. Failures must be repeated in the next cycle, and students on academic probation must show improvement to avoid withdrawal.

2.4 Examination Procedures and Integrity Policy

Examinations are conducted to assess knowledge, critical thinking, and clinical readiness. Eligibility requires 75% attendance, complete assessments, and financial clearance.

Types of Exams: Semester Exams, Continuous Assessments (CAs), Clinical OSCEs, Mock NMCN Boards

Rules:

  • Arrive 30 minutes early
  • No phones or unauthorized materials
  • Present a valid ID
  • Silence and decorum must be maintained

Academic Misconduct Includes: Cheating, impersonation, falsifying records, bribery, and use of unauthorized devices. Penalties range from paper cancellation to expulsion.

2.5 Leave of Absence, Withdrawal, and Readmission

Leave of Absence (LOA): Granted for serious health, family, or national service issues. Requires a formal application, documentation, and Academic Board approval.

Voluntary Withdrawal: Students may exit the program at any point. They must submit a letter, clear dues, and return College property. Readmission later is possible but not guaranteed.

Readmission: Requires reapplication, documentation, and approval. The student may be asked to repeat or audit previous coursework and meet current curriculum standards.

2.6 Disciplinary Process and Sanctions

Principles: All disciplinary action is guided by fairness, proportionality, transparency, and due process. Sanctions are corrective, not punitive.

Categories of Misconduct:

  • Minor: Lateness, noise, incomplete uniforms
  • Major: Absenteeism, insubordination, falsification
  • Gross: Malpractice, theft, harassment, drug use, impersonation

Procedure: Complaint → Inquiry → Hearing → Sanction → Appeal. All outcomes are documented in student records. Students returning from suspension must sign a Reintegration Agreement and may be referred to counseling.

Section 3: Clinical Experience and Requirements

Clinical education is the heart of nursing and midwifery training. It provides students with hands-on experience, allowing them to integrate theory with practice in real-world healthcare environments. At Shajid Royal College of Nursing and Midwifery, clinical experience is structured, progressive, and supervised, ensuring students build competence, confidence, and ethical sensitivity in patient care.

The College partners with government and private hospitals, primary healthcare centers, psychiatric facilities, maternity clinics, and community health programs to offer comprehensive clinical exposure across the lifespan—from neonates to geriatrics.

3.1 Overview of Clinical Training Philosophy

The College adopts a Competency-Based Clinical Education (CBCE) model, aligned with NMCN’s standards and WHO recommendations. This model emphasizes the demonstration of knowledge, skill, and judgment in real-time clinical scenarios.

  • Translate classroom learning into patient-centered care.
  • Develop technical and interpersonal skills required in nursing and midwifery.
  • Promote decision-making, communication, and documentation practices.
  • Foster empathy, ethics, and cultural competence in all care settings.

Students are introduced to clinical practice early in the program and progress from observation to direct care under supervision. Clinical hours are strictly tracked and must be completed in accordance with national curriculum requirements.

3.2 Clinical Placement Process and Supervision

The Clinical Coordinator is responsible for the planning, scheduling, and monitoring of all clinical postings. Students are assigned to accredited institutions based on level of training, program requirements, and departmental availability.

Placement Process:

  • Clinical schedules are released at the beginning of each semester.
  • Students are issued posting letters, which must be presented to the host facility.
  • Each student is attached to a clinical preceptor or ward nurse for supervision.
  • Weekly and end-of-posting evaluations are conducted by both College faculty and hospital staff.

Students are required to:

  • Report to duty on time, in full uniform, and with their logbook.
  • Observe hospital protocol, chain of command, and infection control procedures.
  • Perform nursing interventions appropriate to their level of training.
  • Record daily activities in the Clinical Logbook, which must be countersigned by a supervisor.

Failure to report for clinical duty without valid excuse is treated as a serious academic and ethical violation.

3.3 Health Clearance and Immunization Requirements

To protect both students and the clients they serve, the College mandates a complete pre-clinical health screening before students are cleared to participate in any patient care activity.

Required Medical Tests:

  • Hepatitis B screening and vaccination (mandatory)
  • Tuberculosis (Mantoux or Chest X-ray)
  • HIV testing (voluntary, confidential)
  • Typhoid and Tetanus vaccination
  • Full physical examination
  • Blood group and genotype

A Medical Fitness Certificate from the College-approved hospital must be submitted to the Medical Unit before beginning the first clinical posting.

Students are expected to maintain their health and report any infectious illness, injury, or condition that may compromise patient safety. Pregnant students are encouraged to notify the Clinical Office for appropriate assignment modifications, especially in maternity and theatre units.

3.4 Uniform, Identification, and Personal Grooming Standards

Uniforms are an essential part of clinical professionalism. All students must adhere to the approved uniform code during clinical rotations and practical demonstrations.

Uniform Guidelines:

  • General Nursing: White tunic with College badge and name tag, navy blue skirt or trousers, white closed shoes, nurse’s cap (female) or neat haircut (male).
  • Midwifery: Peach/lavender tunic, white skirt/trousers, white shoes, midwife’s cap with appropriate band.
  • Psychiatric Nursing: Light grey tunic, dark green skirt/trousers, minimal jewelry, flat shoes.

Grooming Standards:

  • Hair must be neat, natural-colored, and off the collar.
  • Fingernails must be short and clean (no polish).
  • No artificial lashes, long braids, or heavy makeup.
  • One pair of small stud earrings (females only).
  • Facial hair must be trimmed or shaved (males).
  • Strong perfumes, chewing gum, and loud conversations are prohibited in wards.

Any student found in non-compliance may be asked to leave the clinical area and reported for disciplinary action.

3.5 Clinical Attendance and Evaluation

100% attendance is required for all clinical postings unless excused for valid reasons. Punctuality and presence reflect the values of trust and responsibility inherent in healthcare.

A. Clinical Attendance Policy

  • Requirement: 100% of clinical hours must be completed. Missed hours must be made up.
  • Punctuality: Students must report 15 minutes before assigned shift time.

Excused Absences: Certified illness, bereavement, civic/legal duty. Documentation is required.

Unexcused Absences: Two unexcused absences = automatic failure of the posting.

B. Clinical Logbooks and Documentation

  • Filled out daily and signed by supervisors.
  • Reflect honest, accurate records.
  • Falsification leads to disciplinary action and possible graduation delay.

C. Clinical Performance Evaluation

  • Knowledge Application
  • Technical Skills
  • Professionalism
  • Critical Thinking
  • Documentation
  • Patient Interaction

Grading Scale: Excellent (80–100%), Very Good (70–79%), Good (60–69%), Fair (50–59%), Fail (<50%)

Students must pass all clinical postings to proceed in the program and qualify for final examination.
