Basic Midwifery Program

The Community Midwifery Program at Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery is a specialized, professionally accredited course designed to train students in maternal and newborn healthcare at the grassroots level. Emphasizing community-based services, essential midwifery skills, and culturally sensitive care, this program prepares future midwives to promote safe motherhood and improve maternal and child health outcomes within their communities.

At Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery, the Basic Midwifery Program is designed to develop skilled, compassionate, and culturally sensitive midwives. Upon successful completion of the program, graduates will be able to:

  • Demonstrate in-depth knowledge of reproductive anatomy, physiology, and maternal health.
  • Provide safe and effective care during pregnancy, labor, childbirth, and the postpartum period.
  • Monitor fetal and maternal well-being using appropriate tools and techniques.
  • Support and educate women and families on birth preparedness and newborn care.
  • Perform essential midwifery procedures with competence and confidence.
  • Promote maternal and neonatal health through preventive care and health education.
  • Communicate effectively and compassionately with women, families, and healthcare teams.
  • Uphold ethical principles and legal standards as defined by the NMCN for midwifery practice.
  • Collaborate with other healthcare professionals to ensure continuity of care.
  • Demonstrate leadership and advocacy in maternal and child health services.
  • Apply research findings and evidence-based practices to improve midwifery outcomes.
  • Prepare for national licensure and commit to lifelong learning in midwifery.

Course Description

The Basic Midwifery Program at Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery is a comprehensive, three-year full-time professional program designed to train skilled, compassionate, and ethically grounded midwives. This program prepares students to support women throughout pregnancy, childbirth, and the postpartum period, ensuring the health and safety of both mother and newborn.

Aligned with the standards of the Nursing and Midwifery Council of Nigeria (NMCN), the curriculum encompasses core biomedical sciences, midwifery theory, reproductive and child health, as well as supervised clinical practice. Students gain the competence to deliver evidence-based care, conduct safe deliveries, manage obstetric emergencies, and provide essential newborn care in both hospital and community settings.

Throughout the program, students undergo clinical rotations in maternity wards, antenatal clinics, primary healthcare centers, and family planning units. The course emphasizes leadership, effective communication, cultural sensitivity, and public health awareness, equipping graduates to meet the unique needs of women and families across diverse environments.

Upon successful completion and licensure by the NMCN, graduates are awarded the Registered Midwife (RM) qualification and are fully prepared to serve in various maternal and child health care roles within Nigeria and globally.

Program Duration: 3 years (6 semesters)

Award: Registered Midwife (RM) Certificate. Approved by the Nursing and Midwifery Council of Nigeria (NMCN)

Admission Requirements:

  • Minimum of five (5) credits in English Language, Mathematics, Biology, Chemistry, and Physics
  • Results accepted: WAEC, NECO, NABTEB (max. two sittings)
  • Candidates must be at least 17 years old at the time of admission

Program Highlights:

  • Hands-on training in antenatal, intrapartum, and postnatal care
  • Skilled faculty and supervised clinical experience in maternity settings
  • Exposure to community-based maternal and child health programs
  • Emphasis on safe motherhood and neonatal care practices
