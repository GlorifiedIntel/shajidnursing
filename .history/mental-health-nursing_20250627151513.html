Mental Health Nursing Program

The Mental Health Nursing Program at Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery is a specialized, professionally accredited course designed to train compassionate and skilled mental health professionals. Focusing on psychological well-being, therapeutic communication, and evidence-based interventions, the program equips students to support individuals facing mental health challenges and to promote emotional resilience within communities.

Mental Health Nursing Program: Course Objectives

At Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery, the Mental Health Nursing Program equips students with the knowledge, skills, and compassion required to provide specialized care for individuals facing mental health challenges. The program emphasizes holistic, ethical, and evidence-based approaches to promote psychological well-being across all age groups. Upon successful completion of the program, graduates will be able to:

  • Understand the biological, psychological, and social factors influencing mental health and illness.
  • Assess, diagnose, and plan individualized care for clients with mental health conditions.
  • Promote mental wellness and recovery through therapeutic communication and counseling.
  • Provide safe, empathetic, and ethical care in acute and community mental health settings.
  • Use behavioral and cognitive strategies to manage psychiatric symptoms.
  • Engage families and caregivers as partners in mental health support and recovery.
  • Document and evaluate mental health interventions in accordance with professional standards.
  • Apply legal and ethical principles related to mental health care and patientsâ€™ rights.
  • Maintain safety, confidentiality, and dignity in all mental health nursing interactions.
  • Collaborate with multidisciplinary teams for integrated mental health services.
  • Incorporate research findings and evidence-based practice into mental health care delivery.
  • Engage in lifelong learning and advocacy for improved mental health awareness and policy.

Course Description

The Mental Health Nursing Program at Shajid College of Nursing and Midwifery is a specialized, professionally accredited course designed to prepare nurses to provide quality care for individuals facing mental, emotional, and behavioral health challenges. The program emphasizes holistic, ethical, and evidence-based approaches to mental healthcare delivery.

In line with the standards of the Nursing and Midwifery Council of Nigeria (NMCN), the curriculum incorporates psychological sciences, psychiatric nursing principles, therapeutic communication, clinical mental health practice, and community mental health education. Students are trained to assess mental health conditions, implement therapeutic interventions, and advocate for individuals with mental health needs.

Students gain hands-on experience through supervised clinical postings in psychiatric hospitals, general hospitals, and community mental health centers. The program also cultivates empathy, resilience, critical thinking, and leadership to address mental health challenges across diverse populations.

Graduates of the program are awarded the Registered Psychiatric Nurse (RPN) qualification upon successful completion and licensure by the NMCN.

Program Duration: 3 years (6 semesters)

Award: Registered Psychiatric Nurse (RPN) Certificate. Approved by the Nursing and Midwifery Council of Nigeria (NMCN)

Admission Requirements:

  • Minimum of five (5) credits in English Language, Mathematics, Biology, Chemistry, and Physics
  • Results accepted: WAEC, NECO, NABTEB (maximum of two sittings)
  • Candidates must be at least 17 years old at the time of admission

Program Highlights:

  • Specialized training in psychiatric and mental health nursing
  • Supervised clinical practice in psychiatric hospitals and mental health centers
  • Emphasis on therapeutic communication and patient advocacy
  • Integration of psychological theory and evidence-based mental health care
